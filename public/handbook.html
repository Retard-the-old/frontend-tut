Welcome to Tutorii

What Tutorii is. How you earn. Then we'll move on.

In one paragraph

Tutorii is a UAE learning app — courses on visas, work rights, money, and jobs. It costs AED 95 a month. When a friend signs up using your link, you earn AED 38 every month they stay. Three friends and your own fee is covered. After three, you earn. This handbook teaches you how to share it well.

You also get your own link to share. When a friend signs up using your link, you earn AED 38 a month — every month they stay. This guide is mostly about that.

First worries — answered up front

Is this MLM?

No. Only two layers — the friends you bring in and one layer below them. Nothing deeper. No buying stock. No joining fee.

Will I lose money?

The most you can ever lose is one month's fee — AED 95. You can stop any time from your account.

Is it real?

UAE company. Real courses. An AI tutor. Payouts run through MamoPay — a UAE Central Bank licensed payment company. Look at www.tutorii.com.

When do I get paid?

Every Tuesday, straight to your UAE bank account. Lands within 1–3 working days. Section 5 covers the details.

How earning works

How you earn money. The simple version.

You can get paid every Tuesday for sharing Tutorii with people you know. Here's how it works:

Tutorii costs AED 95/MONTH. When a friend signs up using your link, you earn AED 38 every month — for as long as they keep paying. Your friend gets the same deal: their own link, their own AED 38 for every friend they share with.

3 friends and your fee is paid for

Get 3 friends to sign up and you cover your AED 95 monthly fee. From the 4th friend onwards, the AED 38 each month is profit.

The maths
  • 3 friends × AED 38 = AED 114 a month back

The honest version

Most people who actively refer get 2–3 friends signed up in their first week — that's your membership effectively covered. There's no cap, but earnings take consistent effort, not luck.

How you get paid

Every Tuesday. Through MamoPay. Straight into your bank.

Lands every Tuesday. Min AED 50. Arrives 1–3 working days.
Setting up your bank details
  1. Log in to www.tutorii.com
  2. Go to Settings
  3. Type in your UAE bank account number (called your IBAN). Payments go to UAE bank accounts only, in AED.
  4. Save. You're done.
When you get paid
  • You get paid every Tuesday
  • The smallest amount they pay out is AED 50 — anything less rolls to next week
  • The money lands in your bank in 1 to 3 working days
  • Always paid in AED — your bank changes the currency if needed
When you get paid · Tuesday is payday

If a payment doesn't go through

Usually it means your bank account number is wrong. Update it in Settings — they'll try again next Tuesday. Email support@tutorii.com if it keeps failing.

Tax

If you live in the UAE, you don't pay income tax on this. If you pay tax in another country, ask a tax adviser. Tutorii doesn't take any tax out and doesn't send tax forms.

Take your money out before you stop

If you stop your Tutorii membership, any money you have under AED 50 is left behind — you can't get it back. So always make sure your money has been paid out before you stop.

Chapter 2 Who to share with and where
Section 06~2 min read

Who to talk to first

Don't message strangers. Message friends first.

TIER 1 People you know well

Close friends. Family living in the UAE. Workmates you trust. People who'd pick up your call late at night.

How easy · Easy How often they say yes · 1 in 3 to 1 in 5
TIER 2 People you sort of know

People you've met but aren't close to. Gym people. Neighbours. Old workmates. People in WhatsApp groups you're already in.

How easy · Medium How often they say yes · 1 in 10 to 1 in 15
TIER 3 Strangers

People you don't know at all. Facebook groups. Reddit. Bigger expat communities online.

How easy · Hard — needs more messages How often they say yes · 1 in 30 to 1 in 50

Always start with Tier 1. Talking to people you know teaches you what to say. By the time you reach Tier 3, you'll know which lines work.

Who responds well in the UAE

  • New arrivals — want to learn how things work here
  • People between/changing jobs — want extra income
  • Parents — want kids to know UAE basics + side income
  • Students and recent graduates — need both
  • People who post about side hustles or freelancing

Who NOT to message first

  • Your boss or senior management at your job
  • Anyone you're trying to impress professionally
  • People who'll judge you for asking
  • People going through a hard time financially
The rule

Start where the stakes are low. If a Tier 1 contact says no, you'll laugh about it next week. If your boss says no, it's awkward for months.

Section 07~4 min read

Where to share

Pick 2 or 3 places to share. Stick with them. Done.

PLACE 1 WhatsApp groups — your best place

Which groups: family groups, country-based groups (Indian, Filipino, Pakistani, Arab, Egyptian, Nigerian groups), neighbourhood groups, school parent groups, hobby groups (football, hiking, book clubs).

How to do it: Don't post your link straight away. Be a normal person first. Wait for someone to talk about visas, jobs, or money — then mention it. Or share a short personal note. Don't post the same message in 5 groups on the same day.

A sample group post
Quick share for anyone interested — there's a UAE app called Tutorii where you earn AED 38 every month for every friend who joins through your link.11:18
It costs AED 95 a month (lessons on visas, your rights at work, money, jobs). But 3 friends covers your fee. After that, you earn.11:18
Send me a private message if you want my link. I earn AED 38 a month if you sign up through me — wanted to be honest. Anyone interested?11:18
PLACE 2 Facebook groups

Which groups: "Indians in Dubai", "Filipinos in Abu Dhabi" (search your country + city), parents' groups, jobs groups, "New to Dubai" groups.

Read the group rules first — some don't allow links. If a group doesn't allow links, post something useful and offer your link by private message instead.

PLACE 3 Instagram

Works if you have UAE followers. Share Stories about specific lessons (visa runs, end-of-service pay), put posts up that say "Send me a message" at the end. Use tags like #DubaiExpats #LifeInDubai #UAEExpats #DubaiJobs.

PLACE 4 Reddit

r/dubai, r/abudhabi, r/UAE, r/sharjah don't allow links — posting one will get you banned. Help people by answering their questions (about visas, end-of-service pay, jobs), and mention Tutorii without a link. If anyone messages you, send the link in the message.

PLACE 5 In person

Often the best way — people already trust you. The gym between sets, school gates, weekend brunches, lobby chats. Don't try to sell. Just mention what you do. If they're interested, send your link on WhatsApp afterwards — they'll click it more often than if you give it to them in person.

PLACE 6 LinkedIn

Be careful here — it can hurt how people see you at work. Don't message strangers your link. Do post sometimes about UAE topics (work rights, visas) and mention Tutorii at the end. The people who reach out to you are the ones to follow up with.

Pick 2 or 3 places. Not all of them.

People who do well pick 2 or 3 places and post in them every week for a few weeks. People who fail try 8 places for 3 days each, then give up. Stick with what you pick.

Section 12~2 min read

Your first 7 days

7 days. In order. Don't skip any.

DAY 1

Get ready

Find your link. Write down 20 names — friends, family, workmates, neighbours, anyone you've messaged in the last 6 months. Don't message anyone yet.

DAY 2

Start sending

Send Message 1 (the friendly opener) to your 5 closest friends. That's it. Wait for replies.

DAY 3

Reply to people

Reply to anyone who wrote back. Don't chase the others yet — give them another day. If anyone asked for more info, use Message 6.

DAY 4

Nudge and add more

Send Message 4 (a 24-hour nudge) to anyone from Day 2 who hasn't replied. Send Message 1 to your next 5 closest friends.

DAY 5

Try a group post

Pick one WhatsApp group you're already in. Post Message 3. Just one group. Answer interested people in private messages.

DAY 6

Follow up

Follow up with people who replied but went quiet — use Message 5. Send Message 1 to 5 more close friends.

DAY 7

Wrap up and plan ahead

Wrap up any open chats. For anyone who's signed up, send the welcome message (see Section 10 — When someone says yes). Plan week 2 — write down 20 more names.

What to expect after week 1

Following this exactly: 15 messages sent, 5 to 8 replies, 2 or 3 friends sign up and pay. That's AED 76 to AED 114 a month locked in.

Some weeks will be better, some worse. People earning AED 1,000+ a month did this same loop for 8 to 12 weeks straight. It's boring and repetitive — but it works.

Section 13~5 min read

Common questions, quick answers

Tap a question to see the answer.

I don't have time to study

The lessons are short — most are 10 minutes. And you don't have to do them to earn money. Your friends pay you whether you watch the lessons or not.

What's the catch?

There's no catch. Tutorii is real. The money is real. The only thing to know: you only get paid as long as your friends keep paying. So pick friends who will really use it.

Can I see what's inside before I pay?

Yes. Go to www.tutorii.com — you can see all the course names and what each lesson covers. The full lessons unlock once you sign up.

What if I want to stop?

You can stop any time from your account. You won't be charged the next month. Take any money you've earned out before you stop — anything under AED 50 stays in the system.

I don't want to give them my card details

Card payments go through a company called MamoPay. They have a UAE Central Bank licence. Tutorii never sees or stores your card details.

Is the stuff inside any good?

The visa rules and workers' rights lessons are the most useful. The money one is good too. The AI tutor is really helpful when you have a quick question.

My friends won't be interested

You'd be surprised. New arrivals, people changing jobs, parents, anyone who could use extra money — that's most people. Try with 3 friends and see what happens.

I'm not good at selling

You don't need to be. Just send people your link with a short note. The website explains everything itself. People who do well don't sell — they just share.

What if Tutorii shuts down?

Tutorii is a registered UAE business. They pay you through MamoPay every Tuesday — MamoPay has a UAE Central Bank licence. You can stop paying any month — so the most you can lose is one month's payment.

I don't know many people in the UAE yet

Then this might suit you well. Your link works on social media, in groups, and in person. Lots of people started here knowing no one and built up over time.

Can I just pay for the courses without sharing my link?

Yes. The referral side is included but you can ignore it. Many people sign up just for the courses. But if you do share your link, the money side adds up fast.

What if my friend stops paying?

You stop earning from them that month. If they sign up again later, you keep them — they're linked to you forever. So pick people who will actually use it.

Do I pay tax on this money?

If you live in the UAE, no — there's no income tax here. If you pay tax in another country, ask a tax adviser. Tutorii doesn't take tax out or send tax forms.

When do I get paid?

Every Tuesday, straight to your bank. The smallest payout is AED 50 — anything less rolls to next week. Money usually arrives 1 to 3 working days after Tuesday.

Will I have to ask my friends for money?

No. Your friends pay Tutorii directly — not you. You never handle their money. Tutorii pays you separately every Tuesday.

What if I'm too busy one month?

Nothing happens. There's no minimum. No targets. No penalties. Your friends keep paying you every month, whether you message anyone new or not.

Is my information safe?

Tutorii follows UAE data protection rules. Your card details are handled by MamoPay — never stored by Tutorii. Your personal information isn't sold to anyone.

Does my friend need to finish the courses for me to get paid?

No. You get paid the moment they sign up and pay. They never need to watch a single lesson. You keep earning every month they keep paying.

Can I share with family? My partner?

Yes — anyone you know, as long as they have their own real account. Don't try to sign yourself up twice or use fake accounts. Tutorii will close your account if they find out.

Can I speak to a real person at Tutorii?

Yes. Email support@tutorii.com — real people answer, Sunday to Thursday, 9am to 6pm UAE time.
